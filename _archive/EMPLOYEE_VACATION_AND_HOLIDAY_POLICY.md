# DML Electrical Service, LLC
## Vacation and Holiday Pay Policy

**Effective Date:** January 2, 2026

---

## Eligibility

All vacation and holiday benefits begin **after one (1) full year of employment** from your hire date.

**Example:** If you were hired on June 15, 2024, you become eligible for vacation and holiday pay on June 15, 2025.

---

## Vacation Time

### Annual Allotment
- **40 hours per year** (equivalent to 5 working days)
- Vacation time resets annually on your hire date anniversary
- **Unused vacation time does not carry over** to the next year

### How It Works
1. **Year 1:** No vacation time available
2. **Year 2 and beyond:** 40 hours available each year on your anniversary date

### Requesting Time Off
1. Submit your request through the Time Clock app or web portal
2. Go to **Settings → Time Off Requests**
3. Select your dates and hours needed
4. Admin will approve or deny your request
5. Once approved, vacation time is automatically scheduled in your timesheet

### Important Notes
- Vacation hours are **deducted automatically** as each vacation day passes
- You can track your available, used, and remaining vacation hours in the app
- Requests should be submitted in advance when possible
- 8 hours = 1 full day

---

## Paid Holidays

### Company Holidays
DML Electrical observes **five (5) paid holidays** per year:

1. **New Year's Day** (January 1)
2. **Independence Day** (July 4)
3. **Labor Day** (First Monday in September)
4. **Thanksgiving Day** (Fourth Thursday in November)
5. **Christmas Day** (December 25)

### Holiday Pay
- **8 hours paid** per holiday
- Automatically added to your timesheet
- **No clock-in required** on holidays
- Included in your regular paycheck

### Eligibility
- Must have completed **one (1) full year of employment**
- Must be an active employee at the time of the holiday
- Part-time employees: Holidays are pro-rated based on typical hours worked

---

## Working on a Holiday

If you are required to work on a company holiday:
- You will receive your regular holiday pay (8 hours)
- **Plus** your regular hourly rate for hours actually worked
- Example: Working 8 hours on Christmas = 16 hours total pay

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When do I become eligible for benefits?
One year after your hire date. For example, hired June 15, 2024 → eligible June 15, 2025.

### What if I don't use all my vacation?
Unused vacation time expires on your anniversary date each year. Plan ahead to use your time!

### Can I request partial days?
Yes! You can request vacation in half-day (4 hours) or hourly increments.

### How far in advance should I request time off?
We recommend submitting requests at least 2 weeks in advance when possible, though we understand emergencies happen.

### What if my request is denied?
Your supervisor will provide a reason. You can submit a request for different dates.

### Do I get paid for holidays if I'm on vacation?
If a company holiday falls during your vacation, you'll receive holiday pay for that day and it won't count against your vacation hours.

### What happens if a holiday falls on a weekend?
The company will observe the holiday on the nearest weekday (typically Friday before or Monday after).
