# Why is a Deposit a DEBIT? (Bank Account Explanation)

## The Short Answer
A **deposit is a DEBIT** because your bank account is an **ASSET**, and for assets:
- **DEBIT = Increase**
- **CREDIT = Decrease**

## The Long Explanation

### Your Perspective vs. Bank's Perspective
The confusion happens because banks show deposits and credits from **THEIR perspective**, not yours.

**Your Bank Statement (from the bank's perspective):**
- Deposit = CREDIT (the bank has to give you more money)
- Withdrawal = DEBIT (the bank gives you less money)

**Your Accounting Books (from YOUR perspective):**
- Deposit = DEBIT (your asset increases)
- Withdrawal = CREDIT (your asset decreases)

### The Rule: Account Type Determines Debit/Credit

| Account Type | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| **ASSET** (Bank Account, Cash, Equipment) | **Increase** ↑ | Decrease ↓ |
| LIABILITY (Credit Card, Loans) | Decrease ↓ | **Increase** ↑ |
| EQUITY (Owner's Investment) | Decrease ↓ | **Increase** ↑ |
| INCOME/REVENUE | Decrease ↓ | **Increase** ↑ |
| EXPENSE | **Increase** ↑ | Decrease ↓ |

### Real Example from Your Account

**When you receive $1,147.63 payment for Invoice #1003:**

```
Bank Account (Asset):
  DEBIT: $1,147.63 ← Increases your asset
  
Revenue/Income:
  CREDIT: $1,147.63 ← Increases income
```

This matches your journal entries:
- Total Debits: $11,378.87 (all the money coming IN)
- Total Credits: $830.07 (all the money going OUT)
- Balance: $11,378.87 - $830.07 = **$10,548.80** ✓

## Why Not Just Use "Income" and "Expense"?

Double-entry accounting requires EVERY transaction to have two sides:
- One side goes to your bank account (DEBIT because it's an asset)
- Other side goes to income/expense/equity accounts (CREDIT)

This ensures the accounting equation always balances:
**ASSETS = LIABILITIES + EQUITY**

## Deposits at Your Bank (the confusing part!)

When you see "$231.24 CREDIT" on your bank statement, the bank is recording:
- **From their books:** CREDIT their liability to you (they owe you money)
- **From your books:** DEBIT your asset (you own that money)

It's the same transaction, just recorded from opposite perspectives!

---

**Bottom Line:** Your account is set up correctly. Deposits are debits because bank accounts are assets, and assets increase with debits. ✓
